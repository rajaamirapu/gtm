Go-To-Market Strategy Learning Module

Time Left: 10:00

Key Strategy Insights

  • Go-To-Market Strategy (GTM) determines how a company delivers its product to target customers and captures value.
  • Marketing begins before product development, identifying customer needs and value opportunities.
  • Customer value occurs when perceived benefit exceeds perceived cost.
  • Products compete not only with direct competitors but also with alternative solutions.
  • Companies must decide:
    • Target segment
    • Value proposition
    • Distribution channel
    • Pricing strategy
  • Need vs Want
    • Need = fundamental problem (e.g., thirst)
    • Want = specific solution (water, juice, bottled water)
  • Brand value influences emotional and functional benefits beyond the product itself.
  • SWOT Analysis evaluates strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
  • Porter's Five Forces helps analyze industry attractiveness.
  • Successful companies deliver multiple elements of value that customers perceive as superior.
